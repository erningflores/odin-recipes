Sinigang na baboy (Pork Soup)

This recipe focuses on the core sour and savory flavors of Sinigang while omitting high-carb ingredients like potatoes and taro. We'll load up on low-carb vegetables instead.


  • Yields: 6-8 servings
  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 1.5 - 2 hours

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs pork belly or pork shoulder, cut into 2-inch cubes
  • 8-10 cups water or pork broth
  • 1 large onion, quartered
  • 3-4 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 2 thumbs-sized ginger, sliced
  • 1-2 stalks lemongrass (tanglad), white part only, bruised and tied into a knot (optional, for Ilonggo flavor)
  • 1-2 cups daikon radish (labanos), peeled and sliced
  • 1 cup green beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch lengths
  • 1-2 cups okra, ends trimmed
  • 2 cups leafy greens (choose one or a combination): spinach, kangkong (water spinach), or pechay (bok choy), roughly chopped
  • 2-3 small eggplants, cut into 1-inch pieces (optional, in moderation)
  • 4-6 tablespoons tamarind paste (adjust to your preferred sourness)
  • Salt or fish sauce (patis) to taste
  • Whole peppercorns to taste
  • Optional: 2-3 green chili peppers (siling haba), for a mild heat

Steps:

  1. Boil the Pork: In a large pot, combine the pork, water (or broth), onion, garlic, ginger, lemongrass (if using), and whole peppercorns
  2. Simmer for Tenderness: Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 1 to 1.5 hours, or until the pork is very tender. Skim off any scum that rises to the surfac
  3. Add Daikon Radish and Green Beans: Add the sliced daikon radish and green beans to the pot. Simmer for about 10 minutes, or until they start to become tender-crisp.
  4. Add Okra and Eggplant (if using): Add the okra and eggplant pieces. Continue to simmer for another 5-7 minutes until they are tender.
  5. Tamarind Paste: Stir in the tamarind paste and adjust the amount to your desired sourness. Simmer for another 5 minutes.
  6. Add Leafy Greens and Chili Peppers (if using): Stir in the chopped spinach, kangkong, or pechay, and the green chili peppers (if using). Cook for just 2-3 minutes until the leafy greens are wilted.
  7. Season: Season the Sinigang with salt or fish sauce (patis) to your taste. Start with a small amount and add more as needed.
  8. Serve Hot: Remove the lemongrass before serving. Ladle the low-carb Sinigang na Baboy into bowls and serve hot with steamed cauliflower rice or enjoy as is.
