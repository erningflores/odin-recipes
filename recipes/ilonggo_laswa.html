Ilonggo Laswa (Vegetable Soup)

Laswa is a simple yet nutritious and flavorful vegetable soup popular in the Ilonggo region of the Philippines. It's a dish that highlights the freshness of local vegetables and is very versatile, allowing for variations based on what's available and in season. Here's a basic recipe to guide you:

  • Yields: 4-6 servings
  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 20-25 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 4-5 cups water
  • 1 small red onion, peeled and sliced thinly
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, peeled and minced (optional, but adds flavor)
  • 1-2 small tomatoes, sliced or quartered
  • 1 cup cubed kalabasa (squash)
  • 1/2 cup cubed gabi (taro root), peeled (optional, but adds thickness)
  • 4-5 pieces medium okra, ends trimmed and cut in half
  • 1 cup sitaw (string beans), cut into 2-inch lengths
  • 1 small patola (sponge gourd), peeled and sliced into rounds (optional)
  • 1 cup saluyot leaves (jute leaves)
  • 1 cup alugbati leaves (Malabar spinach)
  • 1 stalk malunggay leaves (moringa leaves)
  • 1-2 stalks tanglad (lemongrass), white part only, bruised and tied into a knot (optional, for aroma)
  • Salt or fish sauce (patis) to taste
  • Optional: Shrimp (small, whole shrimp or shrimp bouillon cube for added flavor)

Steps:

  1. Boil the base: In a pot, bring the water to a boil.
  2. Add aromatics and root vegetables: Add the sliced onion, minced garlic (if using), sliced tomatoes, cubed kalabasa, and cubed gabi (if using). If using lemongrass, add the tied knot as well. Bring it back to a boil, then lower the heat to a simmer and cook for about 5-7 minutes, or until the kalabasa and gabi start to soften.
  3. Add other vegetables: Add the okra and sitaw. Continue to simmer for another 3-5 minutes, or until they are tender-crisp. If using patola, add it now and cook for another 2-3 minutes until just tender.
  4. Add leafy greens: Add the saluyot, alugbati, and malunggay leaves. Stir gently and cook for just 1-2 minutes until the leaves wilt. Be careful not to overcook the leafy greens.
  5. Season: Season the laswa with salt or fish sauce to your taste. If using shrimp or a shrimp bouillon cube, add it earlier with the kalabasa to allow the flavor to meld into the broth.
  6. Serve hot: Remove from heat and serve hot with steamed rice.
